What is AQI?

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is the metric used for reporting daily air quality. It communicates how clean or polluted your air is, and what associated health effects might be a concern for you.

The AQI focuses on health effects you may experience within a few hours or days after breathing polluted air.

AQI Levels & Colors

  • 0-50 (Good): Little or no risk.
  • 51-100 (Moderate): Acceptable quality.
  • 101-150 (Unhealthy for Sensitive): Risk for some.
  • 151-200 (Unhealthy): Health effects for all.
  • 201-300 (Very Unhealthy): Health alert.
  • 301+ (Hazardous): Health warning.

Health & Safety Measures

Do's

  • Keep indoor plants that purify air (e.g. Snake Plant).
  • Use N95/N99 masks when outside in high AQI.
  • Use HEPA air purifiers indoors if possible.
  • Stay hydrated to help your body naturally flush toxins.

Don'ts

  • Avoid outdoor exercises during high pollution times.
  • Don't keep windows open when outdoor AQI is poor.
  • Avoid walking/exercising near high-traffic areas.
  • Prohibit burning wood, trash, or leaves outdoors.
